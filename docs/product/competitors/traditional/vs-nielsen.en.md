## Real-World Case

**Background**: New beverage brand underperforming in market.

**Using Nielsen (assuming subscribed)**:
- Data shows:
  ```
  Sales: 10K bottles/month (expected 30K)
  Target demographic: Women 18-35
  Channels: Mainly convenience stores
  Competitor comparison: 2% market share (Competitor A 20%)
  ```
- Conclusion: Sales below expectations, but don't know why

---

**Using atypica**:
- Deep interviews with 20 target users (3 hours)
- Findings:
  ```
  Purchasing users (minority):
  - "Nice packaging, healthy, good taste"

  Non-purchasing users (majority):
  User A: "Saw it in convenience stores, but $18 is too expensive.
         Similar products cost $12, not sure if extra $6 is worth it."

  User B: "Packaging is beautiful, but I don't know the difference from competitors.
         Everyone says 'healthy beverage,' what's special about yours?"

  User C: "Convenience stores only have original flavor, I want other flavors.
         Saw on Xiaohongshu there's strawberry, but can't buy it."

  Real problems:
  1. Pricing $18 exceeds psychological expectation ($12-15 appropriate)
  2. Differentiation unclear (healthy is commonality, not difference)
  3. SKU distribution incomplete (convenience stores only 1 SKU)
  ```
- Strategic recommendations:
  ```
  1. Adjust pricing to $15 (improve value perception)
  2. Marketing emphasizes unique ingredient X (differentiation)
  3. Add 2 bestselling SKUs to convenience stores
  4. Xiaohongshu KOL collaboration (increase awareness)
  → Expected: Monthly sales increase from 10K to 40K
  ```
- Post-execution: 3 months reaching 45K monthly sales

**atypica's value**: Nielsen tells you "selling poorly," atypica tells you "why poorly + how to improve."
