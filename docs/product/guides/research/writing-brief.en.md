# Does brief length (simple vs detailed) affect results significantly? How to write good briefs? Are there templates?

## Question Type
User Manual Question

---

## Core Answer

**Impact is minimal, because Plan Mode will help complete and optimize.**

- ✅ Simple description: Plan Mode clarifies details through conversation
- ✅ Detailed description: Plan Mode directly generates precise plan
- ❌ Wrong description: Plan Mode will discover and correct

**Key**: Say clearly what you want to research, leave the rest to Plan Mode.

## What is a Brief?

**Brief (Research Brief)**: A short description of research background and objectives

**Example**:
```
We are a fitness app, recently user churn rate is rising,
want to understand why users churn and how to retain them.
```

**Purpose**:
- Let AI understand background
- Help AI design research direction
- Define research scope

---

## Simple vs Detailed Impact

### Simple Description (Recommended for Beginners)

**Example**:
```
Brief: "Test sparkling coffee new product"
```

**What Plan Mode Does**:
1. Conversation clarification:
   - "Who is your target audience?"
   - "What aspects do you want to understand? Acceptance, pricing, or packaging?"
   - "Any reference materials?"

2. Complete details:
   - Define target user profile
   - Design multiple research questions
   - Select appropriate research methods

**Advantages**:
- Don't need to think too much
- Plan Mode guides your thinking
- Suitable for uncertain situations

**Disadvantages**:
- Requires 3-5 conversation rounds for clarification
- Spend extra 5-10 minutes

---

### Detailed Description (Recommended for Experienced Users)

**Example**:
```
Brief:
"We plan to launch a zero-sugar sparkling coffee, positioned as healthy energy drink,
target users are 25-35 year-old female white-collar workers in tier-1 cities,
main competitors are Starbucks, Manner, and Genki Forest.

Want to understand:
1. How do target users accept the 'sparkling + coffee' combination?
2. Among ¥15/¥18/¥20 pricing options, which is most appropriate?
3. Health positioning vs social positioning, which is more attractive?
4. What should packaging design pay attention to?"
```

**What Plan Mode Does**:
- Directly generate precise research plan
- May only ask 1-2 confirmation questions
- Immediately enter review phase

**Advantages**:
- Save clarification time
- More precise plan
- Higher efficiency

**Disadvantages**:
- Need to think clearly beforehand
- If description is wrong, may deviate direction

## How to Write Good Briefs?

### Template 1: Product Testing Brief

```
Product Background:
- We are [industry] company
- Planning to launch [product name]
- Featuring [core selling point]

Target Users:
- [Age group] [City]
  [Occupation/Characteristics]

Research Objectives:
- Test product acceptance
- Validate pricing strategy
- [Other objectives]

Competitor Situation:
- Main competitors: [Brand A, Brand B]
- Our differentiation: [Differentiation advantage]
```

**Filled Example**:
```
Product Background:
- We are coffee industry startup
- Planning to launch "Sparkling Coffee"
- Featuring zero-sugar health

Target Users:
- 25-35 year-old tier-1 city
  female white-collar workers

Research Objectives:
- Test product acceptance
- Validate pricing strategy
- Collect packaging design feedback

Competitor Situation:
- Main competitors: Starbucks, Manner, Genki Forest
- Our differentiation: Innovative sparkling + coffee combination
```

---

### Template 2: User Insights Brief

```
Business Background:
- Our product/service: [Name]
- Current problem: [Problem description]
- Want to understand: [Core question]

Target Users:
- [User profile]

Research Objectives:
- Understand [certain behavior/attitude]
- Dig into [certain pain point/need]
- Discover [opportunity]

Known Information:
- [Existing data or assumptions]
```

**Filled Example**:
```
Business Background:
- Our product: Fitness App
- Current problem: User churn rate rising to 30%
- Want to understand: Why churn? How to retain?

Target Users:
- Churned users (inactive after 3 months)

Research Objectives:
- Understand real reasons for churn
- Dig into user dissatisfaction pain points
- Discover retention strategies

Known Information:
- Data shows: Churn mainly happens in months 3-4
- Hypothesis: Possibly repetitive content, lack of freshness
```

---

### Template 3: Market Analysis Brief

```
Market Background:
- We want to enter [market/industry]
- Goal: [Market goal]

Research Questions:
- Market size and growth potential?
- Main competitors and competitive landscape?
- Target user needs and pain points?
- Entry opportunities and risks?

Known Information:
- [Existing knowledge]

Constraints:
- Time: [Time constraint]
- Budget: [Budget constraint]
```

**Filled Example**:
```
Market Background:
- We want to enter elderly fitness market
- Goal: Develop fitness products suitable for elderly

Research Questions:
- Elderly fitness market size and growth potential?
- Main competitors and differentiation space?
- Real needs and pain points of elderly users?
- How is willingness to pay?

Known Information:
- Aging trend obvious, elderly health awareness increasing
- Market products mostly designed for young people

Constraints:
- Time: Need to complete within 1 week
- Budget: Medium
```

## Brief Writing Principles

### Principle 1: Clearly State Background and Objectives

✅ **Good Brief**:
```
"We are fitness app, churn rate rising, want to know why churn and how to retain"
```

❌ **Poor Brief**:
```
"Do research"
```

---

### Principle 2: Include Key Information

**Required Information**:
- What is your product/service
- What problem encountered or what to test
- Who are target users

**Optional Information**:
- Competitor situation
- Known data
- Time and budget

---

### Principle 3: Focus on Core Questions

❌ **Too Many Questions**:
```
Want to understand user age, gender, occupation, income, preferences,
purchase channels, usage frequency, satisfaction, recommendation intent...... (20 questions)
```

✅ **Focus on Core**:
```
Want to understand:
1. User acceptance of product
2. Appropriate pricing strategy
3. How to optimize product
```

**Recommendation**: Keep core questions to 3-5

---

### Principle 4: Open Rather Than Closed

❌ **Closed Questions**:
```
"Will users buy?" (Can only answer yes/no)
```

✅ **Open Questions**:
```
"What are users' attitudes toward this product? What factors influence purchase decisions?"
```

**Reason**: Open questions can dig into deeper motivations

## Common Mistakes and Corrections

### Mistake 1: Too Simple

❌ **Wrong Example**:
```
Brief: "Test product"
```

✅ **Correction**:
```
Brief: "Test sparkling coffee new product, target users are 25-35 year-old female white-collar workers,
      want to understand product acceptance and appropriate pricing strategy"
```

---

### Mistake 2: Too Complex

❌ **Wrong Example**:
```
Brief: 3000-word detailed product document + 30 detail questions
```

✅ **Correction**:
```
Brief: 500-word summary of core background and objectives + 3-5 core questions
```

**Reason**: AI will automatically extend details, too complex affects understanding

---

### Mistake 3: Assuming Answers

❌ **Wrong Example**:
```
"Users definitely like our product, right? How to increase sales?"
```

✅ **Correction**:
```
"What are users' attitudes toward our product? What influences their purchase decisions?"
```

**Reason**: Don't assume conclusions, let research discover truth
