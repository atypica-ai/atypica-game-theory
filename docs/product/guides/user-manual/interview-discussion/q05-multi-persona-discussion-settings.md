# How to Have Multiple AI Personas Discuss Together?

## Question Type
User Manual Question

---

## Quick Start (3 Steps)

### Step 1: Create Discussion Project

**Entry Point**:
- Homepage → "Create New Interview"
- Select **"Discussion Chat (Group Discussion)"**

**Fill in Project Information**:
- Project Name: e.g., "Sparkling Coffee Pricing Discussion"
- Research Objective: Brief description of topic you want to explore

---

### Step 2: Select AI Personas (3-8 People)

**Recommended Quantity**: 5-6 people

**Selection Principles**:
- ✅ Ensure viewpoint diversity (different stances)
- ✅ Different backgrounds and consumption psychology
- ❌ Avoid selecting people with similar viewpoints

**Example**: Test sparkling coffee pricing (5 people)
- 2 price-sensitive types (students, working class)
- 2 quality-first types (white-collar, high-income)
- 1 social trendsetter type (Gen Z, Xiaohongshu users)

---

### Step 3: Choose Discussion Type and Start

**3 Discussion Types**:

#### 1. Focus Group - Most Common

**Use For**:
- Product concept testing
- User attitude research
- Feature acceptance

**Characteristics**:
- AI moderator guides discussion
- Free expression of viewpoints
- Seek consensus and divergence

---

#### 2. Debate

**Use For**:
- Solution comparison (A vs B)
- Controversial topic exploration
- Clear pro and con positions

**Characteristics**:
- Divided into pro and con sides
- Opposing viewpoint collision
- In-depth argumentation

---

#### 3. Roundtable

**Use For**:
- Exploratory research
- Open-ended exploration
- No clear stance

**Characteristics**:
- Equal speaking rights
- No preset stance
- Multi-angle insights

---

**Click "Start Discussion"**

---

## Discussion Execution Process

### AI Moderator's Role

**1. Opening and Guidance**:
```
AI Moderator: Hello everyone, today our discussion topic is "Sparkling Coffee Pricing."
First, do you think ¥18 sparkling coffee is expensive?
```

---

**2. Control Speaking Pace**:
- Actively call on speakers: "XX, what's your view?"
- Invite silent ones: "XX hasn't spoken yet, what's your opinion?"
- Control chatterers: "Thank you XX for sharing, let's hear others' thoughts"

---

**3. Guide Viewpoint Collision**:
```
AI Moderator: I notice your price views differ greatly,
A thinks it's expensive, B thinks it's not, can you explain why?
```

---

**4. Deep Dive into Controversies**:
```
AI Moderator: Everyone has different views on the "sparkling + coffee" combination,
some think it's innovative, others think it's a gimmick, why?
```

---

**5. Summarize Consensus and Divergence**:
```
AI Moderator: It seems everyone acknowledges the 0-sugar concept (consensus),
but price acceptance varies greatly (divergence).
```

---

### During Discussion

**Real-Time Viewing**:
- Monitor discussion progress in real-time
- See each persona's statements

**Mid-Way Adjustments**:
- Insert new questions
- Adjust discussion direction
- Extend or end discussion

**Estimated Duration**:
- Standard: 30-40 minutes
- In-depth discussion: 50-60 minutes

## Detailed Real-World Cases

### Case 1: Focus Group - Sparkling Coffee Pricing (5 People)

**Persona Mix**:
- Linda (28, Operations, price-sensitive)
- Emma (32, PM, health-conscious)
- Chloe (26, Designer, social trendsetter)
- Sarah (30, Finance, rational decision-maker)
- Mia (29, Marketing, brand loyalist)

**Discussion Flow**:

**Opening**:
```
AI Moderator: Do you think ¥18 sparkling coffee is expensive?

Linda: Too expensive, I usually buy ¥12 Americano
Emma: I think it's okay, 0-sugar is very attractive
Chloe: Price is acceptable, key is appearance
Sarah: Need to see value, how does it compare to competitors?
Mia: ¥18 isn't expensive, but this brand lacks recognition
```

---

**Deep Dive into Divergence**:
```
AI Moderator: Why do your price views differ so much?

Linda: My budget is limited, ¥18 exceeds daily budget
Emma: I focus more on health, 0-sugar worth paying extra
Chloe: I don't care about price, if I can post on Xiaohongshu it's worth it
```

---

**Explore Consensus**:
```
AI Moderator: What do you think of the 0-sugar concept?

Emma: 0-sugar attracts me, but worried about sweetener safety
Sarah: I also focus on 0-sugar, but need to see ingredients list
Mia: 0-sugar is a trend, this selling point is good

(Consensus: 0-sugar concept is attractive, but need to address sweetener concerns)
```

---

**Key Controversy Point**:
```
AI Moderator: Do you think "sparkling + coffee" is innovation or gimmick?

Chloe: I think it's novel, willing to try
Linda: Sounds weird, worried about bad taste
Emma: Will bubbles irritate stomach?
```

---

**Discussion Results**:
- **Consensus**: 0-sugar concept is attractive
- **Divergence**: Price acceptance varies greatly (depends on income and consumption psychology)
- **Controversy Point**: Is "sparkling + coffee" combination innovative
- **Key Insights**:
  - Need to address sweetener concerns (label "natural sweetness")
  - Price positioning must be clear (¥15-18 range, avoid ¥20+)
  - Packaging design must look good (attract social trendsetters)

### Case 2: Debate - Subscription vs One-Time Purchase (6 People)

**Persona Mix**:
- **Pro** (support subscription): 3 people
  - Small business decision-maker (focus on flexibility)
  - Individual user (don't want to pay too much at once)
  - Heavy user (continuous use, subscription more cost-effective)

- **Con** (support one-time purchase): 3 people
  - Mid-size business decision-maker (focus on total cost)
  - Light user (infrequent use, one-time more economical)
  - Privacy-sensitive (don't want to bind credit card)

**Discussion Flow**:

**Opening**:
```
AI Moderator: Today we discuss subscription vs one-time purchase, which is better.
Pro side believes subscription is superior, con side believes one-time purchase is superior.
First, pro side representative please speak.
```

---

**Pro Arguments**:
```
Pro-Small Business: Subscription is flexible, don't want to pay ¥5000 at once
Pro-Individual: Monthly ¥99 acceptable, one-time ¥2999 too expensive
Pro-Heavy User: I use daily, subscription 2 years only ¥2376, cheaper than one-time
```

---

**Con Arguments**:
```
Con-Mid-Size Business: We use 3 years, one-time ¥2999 lower total cost
Con-Light User: I use few times monthly, monthly ¥99 subscription not worth it
Con-Privacy-Sensitive: Subscription requires credit card binding, I don't want to bind
```

---

**Confrontation**:
```
AI Moderator: Both sides have valid arguments, now please debate freely.

Pro-Heavy User: One-time has no updates, subscription has continuous updates
Con-Mid-Size Business: We don't need frequent updates, stability is good

Pro-Small Business: Subscription can be canceled anytime, one-time has high risk
Con-Light User: Subscription easy to forget to cancel, actually spend more money
```

---

**Discussion Results**:
- **Consensus**: Both models have demand, cannot choose one only
- **Pro Advantages**: Flexibility, continuous updates, low barrier
- **Con Advantages**: Lower total cost, no binding, stability
- **Key Insights**:
  - Offer both options (subscription + one-time)
  - Promote subscription to heavy users, one-time to light users
  - Subscription offers "20% discount for annual payment" to reduce total cost concerns

### Case 3: Roundtable - Explore Fitness App Needs (5 People)

**Persona Mix**:
- Fitness beginner, advanced fitness enthusiast, weight loss group, senior fitness user, rehabilitation trainer

**Discussion Flow**:

**Opening**:
```
AI Moderator: Today we freely discuss fitness app needs and pain points.
What problems do you encounter using fitness apps?
```

---

**Free Discussion**:
```
Beginner: My biggest problem is incorrect form, worried about injury
Advanced: I think app features too basic, not professional enough
Weight Loss: I want to see real-time calorie burn, but app doesn't have it
Senior: Interface too complex, I don't know how to use
Rehabilitation: My knee is injured, app has no suitable training plan for me
```

---

**Moderator Guidance**:
```
AI Moderator: Seems everyone's needs are very different,
beginners focus on ease of use, advanced users focus on professionalism.
If app should prioritize solving one problem, what should it be?
```

---

**Discussion Results**:
- **Consensus**: Form correction is essential need (beginners and seniors both mentioned)
- **Divergence**: Beginners want simple, advanced want professional
- **Key Insights**:
  - Layered design: Beginner mode + Advanced mode
  - Core feature: Form correction AI
  - Differentiation: Personalized training plans (consider injuries)

## Common Questions

### Q1: Can discussion duration be controlled?

**Yes**. You can set:
- Minimum duration: e.g., 30 minutes (ensure sufficient discussion)
- Maximum duration: e.g., 60 minutes (avoid lengthy discussion)
- Auto-judge: AI moderator identifies "viewpoints fully collided" then auto-ends

---

### Q2: Can I add or replace people mid-discussion?

**No**. Cannot replace personas after discussion starts.

**Reasons**:
- Discussion is continuous, mid-way replacement disrupts flow
- New persona lacks context, hard to integrate

**Recommendations**:
- Confirm persona selection beforehand
- Ensure persona diversity

---

### Q3: What if a persona never speaks?

**AI moderator will actively invite**:
- "XX hasn't spoken yet, what's your opinion?"
- Ensure everyone has opportunity to express

---

### Q4: Will discussion deviate from topic?

**AI moderator will control**:
- Identify deviation, pull back promptly
- Ensure discussion focuses on core issues

**You Can Also**:
- Insert new questions mid-way
- Adjust discussion direction

## Best Practices

### Recommendation 1: Ensure Viewpoint Diversity

**Don't**:
- ❌ 5 price-sensitive users (no collision)

**Do**:
- ✅ 2 price-sensitive + 2 quality-first + 1 brand loyalist
- ✅ Ensure opposing viewpoints for discussion value

---

### Recommendation 2: Interview First, Then Discussion

**Best Process**:
1. First use Interview for deep exploration (5-8 people)
2. Discover key controversy points
3. Then use Discussion to observe viewpoint collisions (3-5 people)

**Advantages**:
- Discussion topics more precise
- First understand individuals, then observe groups

---

### Recommendation 3: Use Focus Group to Test Acceptance

**Scenario**: New product concept testing, feature acceptance

**Advantages**:
- Quickly discover consensus and divergence
- Observe viewpoint evolution during discussion

---

### Recommendation 4: Use Debate to Test Solutions

**Scenario**: A/B solution comparison

**Setup**:
- Pro: 3 people support A
- Con: 3 people support B
- Observe which side argues more fully

---

## Final Takeaway

> "Let AI personas discuss together, observe viewpoint collisions,

**Remember**:
- ✅ 3 steps to start: Create project → Select personas → Choose discussion type
- ✅ Ensure viewpoint diversity (different stances)
- ✅ Best practice: Interview deep dive first + Discussion viewpoint collision later
