# Is AI interview based on my pre-set questions for automatic divergence, or just answering these questions? What's the difference from sending out surveys to respondents?

## Question Type
Product Q&A

---

## User's Real Concern

"I've prepared 10 questions. Will AI interview just go through my questions once and that's it? What's the difference from a survey?"

**Core Anxiety**: Worried that AI interviews are just "advanced surveys" without real follow-up and deep exploration.

---

## Core Answer

**AI Interview ≠ Automated Survey. AI interviews involve "intelligent divergence" and "deep follow-up" based on your questions.**

### Core Differences Among the Three

| Dimension | Survey | AI Interview | Human Interview |
|-----------|---------|--------------|-----------------|
| **Follow-up Depth** | ❌ No follow-up | ✅ Unlimited follow-ups | ✅ Limited follow-ups (time constrained) |
| **Question Divergence** | ❌ Fixed questions | ✅ Intelligent divergence based on responses | ✅ Impromptu divergence |
| **Response Depth** | Multiple choice or short answer | 3000+ word deep insights | Depends on interviewer's skill |
| **Execution Efficiency** | 1 hour collection | 3-4 hours (5-10 parallel) | 2-3 weeks (sequential) |
| **Data Quality** | ★★☆☆☆ (Shallow) | ★★★★☆ (Deep) | ★★★★★ (Deepest) |

## 3 Core Capabilities of AI Interviews

### Capability 1: Intelligent Follow-up "Why" (5 Whys Technique)

**Your Initial Question**:
> "Would you buy this ¥18 sparkling coffee?"

**Traditional Survey**:
- [ ] Would buy
- [ ] Wouldn't buy
- [ ] Maybe
- End

**AI Interview Process**:

**Q1**: "Would you buy this ¥18 sparkling coffee?"
- A1: "Probably not."

**Q2 (Follow-up)**: "Why not?"
- A2: "Seems a bit expensive."

**Q3 (Deep dive)**: "Is ¥18 expensive for you? What's your usual coffee budget?"
- A3: "I usually buy ¥12 Americanos."

**Q4 (Comparison)**: "Would you buy it at ¥12?"
- A4: "Still no, mainly because sparkling + coffee sounds weird."

**Q5 (True motivation)**: "Weird? Are you worried about the taste?"
- A5: "Yeah, and during the afternoon, I'm not in the mood to take risks. Coffee is for survival, not experimentation."

**Q6 (Scenario exploration)**: "When would you be willing to try new flavors?"
- A6: "Morning or weekends, when I can afford the risk of disappointment."

**Key Finding**:
- ❌ Surface understanding: Price is too high (¥18 → ¥12)
- ✅ True motivation: **In afternoon survival scenarios, users are unwilling to take the risk of disappointment**

**If using survey**: You only get "probably not," never digging into the true motivation.

---

### Capability 2: Intelligent Divergence Based on Responses

**Your Initial Question**:
> "What do you think about this fitness app's AI personal trainer feature?"

**Traditional Survey**:
- [ ] Very interested
- [ ] Interested
- [ ] Neutral
- [ ] Not interested
- End

**AI Interview Process**:

**Q1**: "What do you think about the AI personal trainer feature?"
- A1: "Sounds good, but I'm worried the AI won't understand my physical condition."

**Q2 (Diverge: Concerns)**: "What aspects worry you?"
- A2: "I have knee problems, worried the AI's recommended movements will worsen my injury."

**Q3 (Diverge: Human experience)**: "Have you worked with a human trainer before? How did they handle your knee issue?"
- A3: "Human trainers adjust movements based on my condition, avoiding high knee-pressure exercises."

**Q4 (Diverge: Expectations)**: "If AI could do this, would you trust it?"
- A4: "If the AI could let me input injury information and automatically adjust the training plan, I'd trust it."

**Q5 (Diverge: Use scenarios)**: "When would you use an AI personal trainer?"
- A5: "When training at home, human trainers are too expensive, but I need supervision and correction."

**Key Findings**:
- Users don't reject AI, they worry "AI won't understand my physical condition"
- Solution: Allow users to input injury information, AI auto-adjusts plan
- Use scenario: Home training, complementing human trainers

**If using survey**: You only get "neutral," unable to discover real needs and solutions.

---

### Capability 3: Identify Vague Responses, Proactively Clarify

**Your Initial Question**:
> "What areas of our product need improvement?"

**Traditional Survey**:
- Open-ended: User fills in "It's okay, nothing special"
- End

**AI Interview Process**:

**Q1**: "What areas of our product need improvement?"
- A1: "It's okay, nothing special."

**Q2 (Identify vagueness)**: "'Okay' means basically satisfied, or some areas unsatisfying but not affecting use?"
- A2: "Well... some features aren't very smooth."

**Q3 (Ask for specifics)**: "Which features aren't smooth?"
- A3: "The note feature is too complex, took me forever to find the save button."

**Q4 (Deep dive pain points)**: "Was it complex the first time, or still complex after several uses?"
- A4: "Used it several times, every time I have to think about how to operate."

**Q5 (Comparison)**: "What note tools do you usually use? What do they do well?"
- A5: "I use the system's native memo app, open and write, saves automatically."

**Key Findings**:
- Note feature's real pain point: Complex operations, doesn't match user habits
- User expectation: Simple and direct, auto-save like memo apps
- Optimization direction: Simplify process, auto-save

**If using survey**: You only get "it's okay," unable to discover real pain points.

## AI Interview vs Survey: Complete Comparison

### Scenario Comparison: New Product Pricing Test

**Research Goal**: Test pricing acceptance for ¥18 sparkling coffee

#### Survey Approach

**Question Design**:
1. Would you buy ¥18 sparkling coffee?
   - [ ] Yes
   - [ ] No
   - [ ] Maybe

2. What do you think is a reasonable price?
   - [ ] ¥10-15
   - [ ] ¥15-20
   - [ ] ¥20+

**Results**:
- 30% would buy, 50% maybe, 20% wouldn't
- 60% think reasonable price is ¥15-20

**Problems**:
- ❌ Don't know "why" 30% would buy
- ❌ Don't know "why" 20% wouldn't
- ❌ Don't know how to optimize product and pricing

#### AI Interview Approach

**Initial Question**:
> "Would you buy ¥18 sparkling coffee?"

**Interview 5 users, deep follow-up**:

**Linda (Price-sensitive)**:
- Won't buy → because expensive → usually buys ¥12 Americano → would buy at ¥12? → still no → why? → **afternoon survival doesn't allow risk**
- **Key insight**: Adjust to morning experimentation scenario

**Emma (Health-anxious)**:
- Maybe → 0 sugar is attractive → but worried about sweeteners → need to check ingredients → sparkling irritates stomach → **health anxiety needs safety**
- **Key insight**: Label "gentle formula, non-irritating"

**Chloe (Social experimenter)**:
- Would buy → high appearance, can take photos → would share on social media → **social currency value**
- **Key insight**: Design attractive packaging

**Results**:
- ✅ Discovered 3 user types with different motivations
- ✅ Found optimization directions: scenario, safety, appearance
- ✅ Actionable improvement plans

## AI Interview Execution Process

### Step 1: You Prepare Initial Questions (3-5)

**Example**:
1. What's your first impression of this product?
2. In what scenarios would you use it?
3. Can you accept the price of ¥X?
4. Compared to competitors, where are the advantages?
5. Any improvement suggestions?

---

### Step 2: AI Intelligently Follows Up Based on Responses

**You don't need to**:
- ❌ Pre-design all follow-up questions
- ❌ Predict what users will say
- ❌ Manually control the interview process

**AI automatically**:
- ✅ Identifies vague responses, follows up for clarification
- ✅ Diverges new questions based on responses
- ✅ Uses "5 Whys" to dig into deep motivations
- ✅ Employs techniques like comparison, hypotheticals, scenario exploration

---

### Step 3: AI Generates Deep Insight Report

**Output Content**:
- Each user's interview summary (3000+ words)
- Wonderful dialogue excerpts as evidence
- Key findings and user personas
- Actionable improvement suggestions

## Common Questions

### Q1: Can I control the depth of AI interviews?

**Yes**. You can set:
- Interview rounds: 5 rounds (quick) vs 10 rounds (deep)
- Follow-up strategy: Focus on core questions vs comprehensive exploration
- End conditions: Stop when goals are met

---

### Q2: Will AI completely deviate from my questions?

**No**. AI interview divergence is "bounded divergence":
- ✅ Always centered around your core questions
- ✅ Reasonably extends based on user responses
- ✅ Won't wander into irrelevant topics

**Example**:
- You ask about pricing, AI won't ask "what music do you like"
- AI will ask: "What's your usual coffee budget?" (relevant)

---

### Q3: Is there a big gap between AI interview and human interview follow-up capabilities?

**Not much, AI is stronger in some aspects**:

| Dimension | AI Interview | Human Interview |
|-----------|--------------|-----------------|
| **Follow-up Consistency** | ★★★★★ (Stable each time) | ★★★☆☆ (Affected by state) |
| **Follow-up Depth** | ★★★★☆ (5-10 rounds) | ★★★★★ (Unlimited follow-ups) |
| **Divergence Ability** | ★★★★☆ (Data-based) | ★★★★★ (Impromptu inspiration) |
| **Emotional Resonance** | ★★★☆☆ | ★★★★★ |
| **Efficiency** | ★★★★★ (5-10 parallel) | ★★☆☆☆ (Sequential) |

**Conclusion**:
- Exploratory research, quick validation → AI follow-up is sufficient
- Deep emotional insights, high-stakes decisions → Human follow-up is better

---

### Q4: When are surveys more suitable than AI interviews?

**Surveys are suitable for**:
- Large-scale data collection (100+ people)
- Simple multiple choice (no need to dig deep)
- Statistical analysis focus

**AI interviews are suitable for**:
- Exploring "why"
- 5-10 people deep insights
- Discovering new problems and directions

## Final Takeaway

> "AI interviews aren't automated surveys, they're intelligent follow-ups and deep divergence.

**Remember**:
- ✅ AI Interview = Your questions + AI follow-ups + Intelligent divergence
- ✅ Survey = Fixed questions, shallow answers
