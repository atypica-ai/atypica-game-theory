## atypica's Unique Value

### 1. User Insights, Not Just Ratings Data

**CTR/CSM's strengths**:
- National TV ratings data (sample households)
- Advertising placement monitoring
- Media value assessment
- Viewing behavior analysis

**CTR/CSM's output example**:
```
Ratings report:
- Program A rating: 2.5%
- Audience profile: Mainly women 35-55
- Time slot: Evening prime time
- Ad value: High

→ Know "how many watch" and "when they watch"
```

**atypica's capability**:
- Understand why audiences watch/don't watch
- Deep interviews dig into viewing motivations
- Validate new content concepts

**atypica's output example**:
```
Deep interviews with audiences:

User A (40-year-old woman):
"CTR data says I'm core audience for this show,
but actually:

I don't 'like' watching, I 'have no choice.'
- 8pm after dinner, accompany parents watching TV
- Parents like this show, so I accompany
- I personally prefer short videos

If you make 'content young people like for families,'
both my parents and I would watch,
not just me 'accompanying.'"

User B (52-year-old woman):
"I do watch this show often (CTR correct),
but recently watching less because:
- Too many ads (15-minute show, 5 minutes of ads)
- Repetitive content (every episode same formula)
- Douyin has highlight clips (3 minutes to finish)

If the show can innovate, I'd continue watching.
But CTR only tells you ratings declining,
not telling you why."

Real insights:
- Ratings data correct (CTR accurate)
- But deep reasons vary:
  * Some audiences are "accompanying" not "liking"
  * Churn reasons: Too many ads + repetitive content + short video impact
- Opportunities:
  * Innovative content formats
  * Content both young and old enjoy
  * Differentiation against short videos
```

**The difference**: CTR tells you "ratings data," atypica tells you "why watch/don't watch + how to improve."

## Real-World Case

**Background**: Variety show ratings declining.

**Using CTR/CSM**:
```
Ratings data:
- Ratings from 3.0% down to 2.0% (-33%)
- Women 35-55 churning most severely
- Competitor show ratings rising

→ Know the data, but don't know reasons
```

---

**Using atypica**:
```
Deep interviews with churned audiences:

User A (42-year-old woman):
"It's not that the show isn't good, but:
- 2 hours per episode is too long, I don't have time
- Douyin has 3-minute highlight clips
- Why watch 2 hours?

If you make 'highlight version' or '45-minute version,'
I'd continue watching."

User B (38-year-old woman):
"Content is repetitive, every episode same formula:
- Guests arrive → games → emotional moment → end
- Got bored after 3 episodes

Competitor show has new creativity every episode,
though production isn't as polished as yours,
at least it's fresh."

Strategic recommendations:
1. Launch "45-minute highlight version" (compete against short videos)
2. Innovative segments every episode (avoid formula)
3. Younger content (attract churned audiences)
→ Post-execution: Ratings recovered to 2.7%
```

**atypica's value**: CTR tells you "ratings declining," atypica tells you "why + how to recover."
