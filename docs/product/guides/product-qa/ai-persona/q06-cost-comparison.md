# Are AI Persona costs really lower than real humans?

## Question Type
Product Q&A (TYPE-A)

## User's Real Concerns
- I heard some channels can find very cheap real people (like students, moms)
- Our company has its own user community, recruitment costs are very low
- AI Personas need subscription fees, are they really more cost-effective?
- If I only do one small-scale interview, which is better?

## Underlying Skepticism
Doubt about AI cost advantage, worried about being misled by "cheap" gimmick

---

## Core Answer

**Real human interview costs vary hugely, can't simply compare "expensive or cheap."**

**Key points**:
- ✅ For **median costs**, AI Personas have competitive advantage
- ✅ Core advantage is not "absolutely cheap," but **"reusability" and "scalability"**
- ✅ One-time creation (may require higher investment), multiple uses (extremely low marginal cost)

**Simply put**: Real human interview costs depend on who you find and through what channels; AI Personas' advantage is "the more you use, the more cost-effective."

---

## Detailed Explanation

### Why Are Real Human Costs Hard to Compare?

**Real human interview costs vary hugely, depending on multiple factors**:

#### Factor 1: Different Target Demographics

```
High-end demographics (difficult to recruit, high cost):
- Corporate executives, doctors, lawyers
- High-net-worth individuals, professional technicians
- Niche interest groups, special professions

Mass demographics (relatively easy, low cost):
- University students, moms, regular office workers
- Common consumer profiles
- Local residents

Cost difference: Can differ by 10x or more
```

**Cases**:
- Want to interview "cosmetic surgery consumers"? Students and moms might be relatively easy to recruit
- Want to interview "enterprise SaaS procurement decision-makers"? Recruitment difficulty and cost will be much higher
- Want to interview "niche hobbyists (like surfing enthusiasts)"? May need very long time and higher cost

---

#### Factor 2: Different Recruitment Channels

```
Private community channels (almost free):
- Your own user base
- Customer WeChat groups
- Membership system
- Cost: Almost 0, but limited scope

Public recruitment (need to pay):
- Professional recruitment platforms
- Wenjuanxing, Diaoyanba, etc.
- Social media ad recruitment
- Cost: Medium to high, but broad coverage

Professional Panel (high cost):
- Third-party professional panel
- Strict quality screening
- Cost: High, but quality assured
```

**Conclusion**: If you have a private community, real human costs may be very low; if you need public recruitment of specific demographics, costs will rise.

---

#### Factor 3: Different Regions and Cities

```
Regional differences:
- Tier 1 cities: Recruitment relatively easy, but cost higher
- Tier 3-4 cities: Recruitment may be difficult, cost medium
- Remote areas: Recruitment very difficult, may need more incentives

Cross-region needs:
- Need users from multiple cities? Cost multiplies
- Need overseas users? Cost and difficulty rise significantly
```

---

#### Factor 4: Different Project Nature

```
Project factors affecting cost:
- Interview duration: 30 minutes vs 2 hours
- Topic sensitivity: Daily consumption vs health privacy
- Professional requirements: Regular users vs industry experts
- Time requirements: Flexible vs specific work hours

Conclusion: More complex projects, higher real human costs
```

---

### AI Persona Cost Logic

**Core is not "cheap," but "reusability"**:

#### Logic 1: One-time Creation, Multiple Uses

```
Real human interviews:
- Need to re-recruit for each research
- Need to pay costs each time
- Cannot "save" interviewees for next time

AI Personas:
- One-time creation (may require higher investment)
- But can be used for multiple research projects
- Extremely low marginal cost

Analogy:
Real human = Taking taxi each time (pay each time)
AI Persona = Buying car (one investment, multiple uses)
```

**Case: Continuous Research on Healthy Snack Users**

```
1st research: Packaging design test
2nd research: Pricing strategy validation
3rd research: New flavor test
4th research: Marketing copy test

Real human method:
- Re-recruit 10-15 people each time
- Pay recruitment costs each time
- 4 researches, 4 costs

AI Persona method:
- 1st time create 10-15 Custom Personas (higher investment)
- Next 3 researches use directly (almost no additional cost)
- More cost-effective with use

Conclusion: 1st time costs may be similar, but 2nd-4th times AI Persona advantage obvious
```

---

#### Logic 2: Scalability Advantage

```
Small scale (5-10 people):
- Real human and AI cost difference not big
- Choice depends on other factors (time, quality, etc.)

Medium scale (20-50 people):
- AI Persona starts showing advantage
- Larger scale, lower marginal cost

Large scale (100+ people):
- AI Persona advantage huge
- Real human recruitment difficulty and cost grow exponentially
- AI has almost no recruitment bottleneck
```

**Case: Testing 50 Product Concepts**

```
Need: 10 people per concept, 500 total interviews

Real human method:
- Recruit 500 people? Almost impossible
- Reuse same batch of people? Causes "fatigue"
- Batch recruitment? Both time and cost very high

AI Persona method:
- Create 50 personas
- Each concept test with 10 personas
- Can flexibly combine and reuse
- Scalability advantage obvious
```

---

#### Logic 3: Rapid Iteration Advantage

```
Scenario needing research direction adjustment:

Real human method:
- Round 1: Recruit demographic A (already paid cost)
- Discover wrong direction, need to change demographic
- Round 2: Re-recruit demographic B (pay cost again)
- Sunk cost: Round 1 recruitment fees cannot be recovered

AI Persona method:
- Round 1: Use persona set A (low cost)
- Adjust direction, switch to persona set B (low cost)
- Can quickly switch and iterate
- Low trial-and-error cost
```

### Competitiveness Against Median Costs

**Our comparison logic**:

```
Don't compare extremes:
❌ Don't compare "free private community" vs "AI subscription" (unfair)
❌ Don't compare "¥10 students" vs "AI interviews" (different quality)
❌ Don't compare "high-end Panel" vs "AI interviews" (different scenarios)

Compare median costs:
✅ Against median recruitment costs (common demographics, public channels)
✅ Include complete costs (recruitment, screening, execution, time)
✅ Consider reuse and scalability factors

Conclusion:
→ Against median costs, AI Personas are competitive
→ Especially in "continuous research" and "large-scale research" scenarios
```

### Real Comparison Scenarios

#### Scenario 1: One-time Small-Scale Research (5-10 people)

```
Real human method:
- If have private community: Very low cost
- If public recruitment: Medium cost
- Time: 1-2 weeks

AI Persona method:
- Use Tier 1 Public Personas: Low cost
- Time: Hours

Conclusion:
- Cost difference not big
- If have private community, real humans may be more direct
- If need quick completion, AI advantage obvious
```

---

#### Scenario 2: Continuous Research Same Demographic

```
Need: Test once per month, continue for 6 months

Real human method:
- Re-recruit monthly or repeatedly interview same batch

- 6 months cumulative cost

AI Persona method:
- 1st month create Custom Personas

- No "fatigue" problem, stable consistency

Conclusion:
- AI Persona advantage obvious
- Months 2-6 almost free
- This is AI Persona's most suitable scenario

Need: Research 5 different demographics, 20 people per demographic

Real human method:

- Some demographics may be difficult to recruit

AI Persona method:

- Cover 5 demographics, no recruitment bottleneck
- Scalability advantage obvious

Conclusion:
- AI Persona large-scale advantage huge
- Real human recruitment difficulty grows exponentially
- AI has almost no recruitment ceiling
```

---

#### Scenario 4: Cross-Regional Research

```
Need: Need Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, Shenzhen + overseas users

Real human method:
- Separate recruitment for each region
- Cross-region cost and difficulty multiply
- Overseas recruitment more difficult

AI Persona method:
- Select personas from different regions
- No geographic cost difference
- Overseas personas also covered

Conclusion:
- AI Persona cross-regional advantage obvious
- No geographic cost difference
```

### Decision Framework: When to Use Which?

#### Prioritize Real Humans

```
✅ Have mature private community, recruitment cost almost 0
✅ One-time research, no need for subsequent reuse
✅ Small-scale research (within 5 people)
✅ Deep emotional excavation (need to observe micro-expressions)
✅ Physical product experience (need to touch, taste)
✅ High-risk decision validation (need real human endorsement)
```

---

#### Prioritize AI Personas

```
✅ Continuous research same demographic (multiple reuse)
✅ Large-scale research (50+ people)
✅ Rapid iteration (need multiple direction adjustments)
✅ Cross-regional research (multiple cities/countries)
✅ Niche demographics (difficult to recruit)
✅ Sensitive questions (need authentic answers)
✅ Exploration phase (direction unclear, trial-and-error cost must be low)
```

---

#### Best Practice: Combined Use

```
Recommended strategy (for medium-scale projects):

Phase 1: AI rapid exploration (50-100 people)
→ Quickly validate hypotheses
→ Discover key insights
→ Filter directions worth diving into

Phase 2: Real human deep validation (10-20 people)
→ In-depth interviews on key findings
→ Observe emotions and micro-expressions
→ Gain real human endorsement and confidence

Value:
→ AI handles "breadth" (large-scale screening)
→ Real humans handle "depth" (key validation)
→ Both total cost and time more optimal
```

### Value Beyond Cost

**Besides cost, AI Personas have other advantages**:

#### Advantage 1: Time Value

```
Real human recruitment:
- Post need → 1-2 days
- Screen applicants → 2-3 days
- Schedule times → 3-5 days
- Execute interviews → 1 week
- Total: 2-3 weeks

AI Persona:
- Select personas → 10 minutes
- Start interview → Immediate
- Complete interview → Hours
- Total: Within 1 day

Value:
- Time is cost
- 2-3 weeks faster may mean capturing market opportunity
```

---

#### Advantage 2: Consistency Value

```
Real human problems:
- Different interviewees, large quality differences
- May encounter uncooperative, perfunctory interviewees
- Same person repeated interviews, will change answers

AI Persona:
- 85 Consistency Score (exceeds real human baseline 81), stable and reliable
- Won't be "perfunctory" or "uncooperative"
- Can interview multiple times, results stable
```

---

#### Advantage 3: Flexibility Value

```
Real human limitations:
- Already recruited people, cannot "change people"
- Direction adjustment needs re-recruitment
- High sunk cost

AI Persona:
- Switch different personas anytime
- Direction adjustment, change immediately
- Low trial-and-error cost
```

## Common Questions

### Q1: If I have private community, cost almost 0, still need AI?

**Depends on your needs**:

```
Private community advantages:
✅ Low recruitment cost
✅ Users are real
✅ Can build long-term relationships

Private community limitations:
⚠️ Sample may be biased (all your existing users)
⚠️ Cannot cover non-user demographics
⚠️ Repeated interviews lead to "professional interviewees"
⚠️ Sensitive questions may not get honest answers

Recommended strategy:
→ Existing user research: Use private community real humans
→ Potential user exploration: Use AI Personas
→ Competitor user research: Use AI Personas
→ Combined use, complementary advantages
```

---

### Q2: Heard some platforms can find ¥10-20 students, does AI still have advantage?

**Depends on research quality requirements**:

```
Low-cost real human problems:
⚠️ Student demographic single, limited representativeness
⚠️ May be perfunctory for money
⚠️ Lacks target demographic's real experience
⚠️ Quality cannot be guaranteed

AI Persona Tier 1 (based on Census data):
✅ Cover diverse demographics
✅ Based on real data, not "fabricated"
✅ Stable consistency
✅ Against median quality, competitive

Conclusion:
- If only need "someone to answer," low-cost real humans may suffice
- If need "target demographic's real feedback," AI Persona quality more assured
- Especially Custom Personas (created from real human interviews), quality close to real humans
```

---

### Q3: One-time research, is AI still cost-effective?

**Depends on scale and demographic**:

```
Small-scale one-time (5-10 people, common demographic):
- Real human and AI cost difference not big
- Choice depends on time requirements
- If urgent, AI faster

Large-scale one-time (50+ people):
- AI scalability advantage obvious
- Even if only used once, still easier than real human recruitment

Special demographic one-time (niche, cross-regional):
- AI recruitment no bottleneck
- Real humans may be difficult to recruit or very high cost
```

---

### Q4: Custom Persona creation cost high, still cost-effective?

**Custom Persona investment logic**:

```
Custom Persona creation:
- Need to upload real human interview records
- Or use Social Media Scanning to observe social media
- One-time creation investment high

But value is:
✅ One-time creation, permanent save
✅ Can be used for multiple research projects
✅ Quality close to real humans
✅ Can share with team members (team version)
✅ Form company's "user assets"

Applicable scenarios:
→ Core target demographics (will research long-term)
→ Team collaboration (multiple people using)
→ Continuous product iteration (multiple tests)

Not applicable scenarios:
→ One-time research
→ Non-core demographics
→ Budget-limited exploration phase
```
