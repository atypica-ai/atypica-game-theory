### 2. AI recommendation reliability

**1. Persona quality assurance**

```
Custom personas (highest quality):
- Based on real interview records or Scout deep observation
- High behavioral consistency
- Applicable scenarios: Key decisions, pre-product launch

Public persona library (standard quality):
- Based on general demographic profiles
- Relatively high behavioral consistency
- Applicable scenarios: Quick validation, direction exploration

Temporary generated personas:

- Applicable scenarios: Concept testing, preliminary exploration

**Usage recommendations**:
- Key decisions → Use custom personas
- Quick validation → Public persona library acceptable
- Preliminary exploration → Temporary generation for rapid trial-and-error

**2. Multi-method cross-validation**

```
Single method:
Only 1 AI Interview → May have bias

Cross-validation (90%+ reliability):
Scout social media
  ↓
Discussion quick group discussion
  ↓
Interview deep one-on-one interviews
  ↓
(Optional) Real interviews to verify key findings
```

**Example**:
```

Method 1 (AI Interview only):
- 5 AI personas feedback: Like design A
- Reliability: 75%
- Risk: Possible bias

Method 2 (cross-validation):
- Scout Xiaohongshu: Design A style popular
- Discussion (8 people): 6 like A, 2 like B
- Interview (5 people): Deep inquiry, confirm A's advantages
- Real testing (optional): Find 3 real people to verify
- Reliability: 90%+
- Risk: Low
```

Every AI recommendation includes:

```
Recommendation: Your product should be priced at ¥299

Reasoning process:

**Value**:

- You can judge if reasoning is sound
- You can see if data is sufficient

#### Case B: Incorrect usage (failure)

**Scenario**: Healthy snack startup

**User behavior**:
```
Step 1: Research with atypica
- Interview 3 AI personas
- AI recommends: Product price ¥28, target users 25-35 year old women

Step 2: Direct execution (no verification)
- See recommendation, immediately price at ¥28
- Didn't understand AI reasoning process
- No cross-validation

Step 3: Full rollout (no testing)
- Immediately produce 10,000 units
- Full channel distribution
- Price ¥28

Result:
- Poor sales (cost ¥25, profit only ¥3)
- Competitor ¥18, own ¥28, lacks price competitiveness
- Inventory backlog
```

**Result**: Failure ❌

**Root cause analysis**:
```
Error 1: Only used 3 AI personas, sample too small
- Correct: Should use Scout + Interview + Discussion

Error 2: Didn't understand AI reasoning
- AI recommended ¥28 based on "quality positioning"
- But user didn't clarify wanting "quality" route
- Also didn't consider own brand power insufficient to support ¥28

Error 3: Didn't consider costs
- Cost ¥25, price ¥28, profit too low
- Should optimize cost or adjust pricing

Error 4: No small-scale testing
- Directly produced 10,000 units, risk too high
- Should first produce 1,000 units to test market feedback

Error 5: Ignored competitors
- Competitor ¥18, own ¥28, needs strong differentiation
- But user didn't establish sufficient brand differentiation
```

**Reflection**:
> "AI recommending ¥28 wasn't wrong, my usage was wrong:
> 1. Sample too small (only 3 AI personas)
> 2. No validation (no cross-validation and testing)
> 3. Didn't combine with own situation (cost, brand power, competitors)
> 4. No small-scale testing (direct full rollout)
>
> Responsibility is mine, not AI's."

### 6. How to reduce risk

#### Risk control checklist

**Research phase**:
```
✅ Use custom personas (highest quality)
✅ Scout real user discussions on social media
✅ Cross-validate with Interview + Discussion
✅ Read AI reasoning process, understand why
✅ Note AI's "knowledge gaps"
```

**Decision phase**:
```
✅ Combine with own situation (cost, brand, resources)
✅ Check competitor and industry data
✅ Find 2-3 real people to verify key findings
✅ Consult professionals (if necessary)
✅ Don't blindly trust any single source
```

**Execution phase**:
```
✅ Small-scale testing (pilot first, then scale)
✅ Monitor key metrics (conversion rate, feedback)
✅ Rapid iteration and adjustment
✅ Record lessons learned
```
