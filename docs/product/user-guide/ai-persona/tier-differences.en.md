# How to Choose the Right AI Persona?

## Question Type
User Manual Question

---

## Quick Answer

**atypica provides two major types of AI personas: Public Persona Library (300,000+) and Custom Personas.**

**Recommended Selection Logic**:
- ✅ **General research**: Use Public Persona Library directly (fast, broad coverage)
- ✅ **Specific needs**: Create custom personas (upload interview data or use Social Media Scout)

**In short**: For most cases, the Public Persona Library is sufficient; only create custom personas when you specifically need to simulate particular real users.

---

## Detailed Explanation

### Public Persona Library: 300,000+ Pre-Built Personas

**Characteristics**:
- **Quantity**: 300,000+ covering diverse demographics
- **Source**:
  - ✅ Built based on real research contexts (each persona corresponds to 1 real research question)
  - ✅ Built based on real social media observations (each persona has 3000-word observation record)
- **Quality**:
  - ✅ Consistency score of 80 (close to real human baseline of 81)
  - ✅ Stable quality, validated
- **Visibility**: Shared by all users (both free and paid versions can use)

**Use Cases**:
```
✅ Product concept testing
✅ Market trend exploration
✅ User attitude research
✅ Creative brainstorming discussions
✅ Quick hypothesis validation
✅ Large-scale demographic research
```

**Examples**:
- Test sparkling coffee new product pricing (select 10-15 target demographics)
- Understand fitness app user needs (select fitness enthusiast personas)
- Explore Gen Z consumption preferences (filter 18-25 age personas)

**Advantages**:
- ✅ Ready to use immediately, no creation needed
- ✅ Broad coverage, virtually any demographic available
- ✅ Stable quality, already validated
- ✅ Low cost, no additional creation cost

---

### Custom Personas: Tailored for Specific Needs

**Characteristics**:
- **Quantity**: Created based on your needs
- **Source**: Based on your provided data (interview transcripts, social media observations)
- **Quality**: Depends on your provided data quality
- **Visibility**: Completely private, only you can use (team version can share with team)

**Use Cases**:
```
✅ Simulate specific real users
✅ Enterprise customer research
✅ Sensitive data research
✅ Niche groups not found in public library
✅ Fixed user groups requiring long-term tracking
✅ Core personas for team sharing
```

**Examples**:
- Import 500 member interview data to test new product (simulate real members)
- Observe "camping enthusiasts" on Xiaohongshu, create personas (not in public library)
- Continuously track fixed user group's changing needs (multiple reuses)

**Creation Methods**:
1. **Upload Interview Transcripts**: Upload real interview text or audio, AI auto-generates persona
2. **Social Media Scout**: Let AI observe target demographics on social media, generate personas

**Advantages**:
- ✅ Fully customized, meets your specific needs
- ✅ Data privacy, protects sensitive information
- ✅ Reusable, forms company's "user assets"
- ✅ Team sharing, improves collaboration efficiency

## Decision Framework: Which Should I Choose?

### Scenario 1: General Product Testing

```
Need: Test new product concept

Recommended: Public Persona Library
Reason:
→ 300,000+ library covers most target demographics
→ Ready to use immediately, saves time
→ Stable quality, reliable results

Steps:
Step 1: Describe target demographic (e.g. "25-35 year old females who like healthy eating")
Step 2: System recommends 10-15 matching personas
Step 3: Select personas, start interviews
```

---

### Scenario 2: Specific Demographics (Not Found in Public Library)

```
Need: Study "surfing enthusiasts" (niche group)

Recommended: Custom personas (Social Media Scout)
Reason:
→ Public library may not have or have insufficient coverage
→ Scout can observe real surfing enthusiasts on Xiaohongshu, Douyin
→ Generate personas that better match real demographics

Steps:
Step 1: Use Social Media Scout function
Step 2: Specify observation target "Surfing enthusiasts on Xiaohongshu"
Step 3: AI observes and generates personas
Step 4: Save to your persona library for future use
```

---

### Scenario 3: Simulate Real Customers

```
Need: Test new features, want to simulate real VIP customers

Recommended: Custom personas (upload interview transcripts)
Reason:
→ Need to simulate specific real users
→ You have historical interview data
→ Need to protect customer privacy

Steps:
Step 1: Upload VIP customer interview transcripts (anonymized)
Step 2: AI generates personas
Step 3: Use personas to test new features
Step 4: Can reuse later without re-interviewing real customers
```

---

### Scenario 4: Team Collaboration Research

```
Need: Product team needs to share core user personas

Recommended: Custom personas (team version)
Reason:
→ Team needs unified user understanding
→ Reuse same personas across multiple studies
→ Form company's "user assets"

Steps:
Step 1: Create custom personas (based on real user data)
Step 2: Save to team persona library
Step 3: Team members can all use
Step 4: Continuously accumulate, more valuable over time
```

## Best Practices

### Recommended Strategy: 80% Public + 20% Custom

```
Most research:
→ Use Public Persona Library (fast, sufficient)
→ Covers common demographics, stable quality

Specific needs:
→ Create custom personas (customized, in-depth)
→ Niche groups, real customer simulation, team collaboration
```

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q1: Is the Public Persona Library quality reliable?

**Reliable, close to real human performance.**

```
Quality assurance:
✅ Built based on real research contexts (300,000+ real research questions)
✅ Built based on real social media observations (each persona has 3000-word observation record)
✅ Consistency score of 80 (close to real human baseline of 81)
✅ 300,000+ library covers diverse demographics
✅ Continuously updated and optimized

Applicable scope:
→ Sufficient for 90% of general research
→ Product testing, market exploration, user research, etc.
```

---

### Q2: When do I need to create custom personas?

**Only needed for specific requirements.**

```
Situations requiring custom:
✅ Public library cannot find matching demographics (niche, special)
✅ Need to simulate specific real users (your customers)
✅ Have sensitive data, need private storage
✅ Team needs core personas for long-term reuse

Situations not requiring custom:
→ Common demographics (25-45 year old professionals, college students, mothers, etc.)
→ General research (product testing, market research)
→ Quick hypothesis validation
```

---

### Q3: Is custom persona creation cost high?

**Depends on your data and usage frequency.**

```
Creation cost:
- Upload interview transcripts: Need interview data (may already have)
- Social Media Scout: Requires Scout quota

But value lies in:
✅ Create once, save permanently
✅ Can be used for multiple research projects
✅ Extremely low marginal cost (more cost-effective with use)
✅ Can share with team (team version)
✅ Form company's "user assets"

Suitable scenarios:
→ Core target demographics (will study long-term)
→ Team collaboration (multiple users)
→ Continuous product iteration (multiple tests)

Not suitable scenarios:
→ One-time research
→ Non-core demographics
→ Public library already has suitable personas
```

---

### Q4: Will custom personas be visible to other users?

**No, completely private.**

```
Privacy protection:
✅ Custom personas only visible to creator
✅ Other users cannot search or use
✅ Data completely isolated
✅ Team version can selectively share with team members

vs Public Persona Library:
→ Public personas: Shared by all users
→ Custom personas: Completely private
```

```
Free version permissions:

Professional/Team versions:

```

### Q6: Can I "bookmark" public personas to my library?

**Yes, use "My Persona Library" function.**

```
Steps:
Step 1: Find suitable persona from public library
Step 2: Click "Add to My Persona Library"
Step 3: Persona appears in your personal library
Step 4: More convenient for future use, no need to search repeatedly

Value:
→ Quick access to frequently used personas
→ Organize and categorize your personas
→ Team can share bookmarked personas
```
