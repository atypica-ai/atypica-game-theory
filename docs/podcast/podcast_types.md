## 1. Case Study Walkthrough with Client (2 speakers):
- one play the role of the solutionist; one play the role of the customer with questions(from the audience's perspective)
### What is this format?
Two casts, one male and one female, jointly present the research as a case study. one focus on methodology and findings, while the other shares decision-making process, implementation challenges, and business context.

### Benefits for audience attraction:
- Complete business story from research to implementation
- Real business stakes make content more compelling
- Two expert perspectives provide comprehensive view
- Practical implementation insights highly valuable to audience
- Authentic business relationship builds trust with potential clients

### How to pull this off:
- Structure as "before, during, after" - problem, research, decision, results
- Assign clear roles - you handle methodology, client handles business context
- Include "behind the scenes" moments and challenges
- Share actual results/outcomes when possible
- Practice the flow to avoid overlap and ensure smooth transitions

### Suitable topic types:
- Any completed project where you can share results
- Strategic decisions with clear business outcomes
- Market entry strategies with measurable results
- Product launches with performance data
- Organizational changes with before/after metrics

### Other considerations:
- Client must be willing to share business details publicly
- Requires project completion - can't use for ongoing work
- Potential competitive sensitivity limits what can be shared
- Builds strongest credibility when results are positive

## 2. Dialogue Interview Format (2 speakers)
- one plays the role of the solutionist; one play the role of domain expert responsible with the business background
### What is this format?
You conduct a structured interview with a key stakeholder from your research - either the client who commissioned the study, a representative user you interviewed, or a domain expert. You play the role of the interviewer, guiding the conversation to reveal insights.

### Benefits for audience attraction:
- Natural conversation flow feels more engaging than monologue
- Multiple perspectives add credibility and depth
- Spontaneous moments create authentic, memorable content
- Question-answer dynamic mirrors how audience naturally processes information
- Credibility boost from having actual stakeholders validate your findings

### How to pull this off:
- Pre-brief your guest with key talking points but leave room for spontaneity
- Prepare transition questions that naturally flow from research phases
- Use the "naive interviewer" technique - ask questions your audience would ask
- Record separately then edit together for better audio quality
- Create a question bank for different guest types (clients, users, experts)

### Suitable topic types:
- Decision-type research (like your Bosch case) - interview the product manager
- Market insight research (golf club market) - interview a key user or industry expert  
- Strategic planning (Nordic person on Xiaohongshu) - interview a successful content creator
- Innovation projects - interview the entrepreneur or innovation lead

### Other considerations:
- Guest availability can delay production
- Need consent/permissions for commercial research topics
- Requires more coordination but creates stronger stakeholder relationships
- Can reveal proprietary info - need careful content control

## 3. Investigative Documentary Style (Solo)
- unravels the whole research process in a premium, professional and story-telling style. Teaching the methodology, resonant with same field business consultants
### What is this format?
You position yourself as an investigative journalist uncovering a business mystery. Heavy use of "found audio" - clips from user interviews, industry reports, competitor analysis, market data - woven together with your narrative.

### Benefits for audience attraction:
- High production value feels premium and professional
- Multiple voices without needing co-hosts
- Suspense and mystery keeps audience hooked
- Authentic user voices add credibility and emotion
- Cinematic feel stands out in business podcast landscape

### How to pull this off:
- Record user interview clips specifically for podcast use during research
- Create "audio scenes" - describe environments, sounds, contexts vividly
- Use transition phrases like "But then I discovered..." or "What happened next surprised me"
- Layer in data reveals as "evidence" in your investigation
- Build to crescendo moments where key insights are revealed

### Suitable topic types:
- Market disruption analysis - "The mystery of why golf club dealers are struggling"
- User behavior studies - "What I discovered in Southeast Asian bubble tea shops"
- Competitive intelligence - "Inside the TikTok seller's playbook"
- Innovation forensics - "How Nordic content creators cracked the Chinese social code"

### Other considerations:
- Higher production time due to editing complexity
- Need quality recordings of all "found audio" elements
- Copyright concerns with third-party content
- Requires strong storytelling skills to maintain momentum
